The Impending Quantum Threat

The global financial system is built on classical encryption like RSA and ECC. These methods are secure today because they rely on mathematical problems that are too complex for even the fastest supercomputers. However, a sufficiently powerful quantum computer running Shor's algorithm could solve these problems in minutes, rendering our entire financial security infrastructure obsolete.

Vulnerability: "Harvest Now, Decrypt Later"

Adversaries can record encrypted financial data today and store it. Once a powerful quantum computer is available, they can decrypt this historical data, exposing past transactions, trade secrets, and sensitive customer information.

Risk: System-Wide Collapse

The ability to break encryption in real-time could allow for fraudulent transactions, market manipulation, and a catastrophic loss of trust in the digital economy, potentially leading to a global financial crisis.

The QKD Shield: A New Paradigm for Financial Security

Forward Secrecy

QKD generates ephemeral keys that are used once and then discarded. Even if a key were compromised, it could not be used to decrypt past or future messages, neutralizing "harvest now, decrypt later" attacks.

Provable Security

The security of QKD is based on the laws of physics, not mathematical complexity. The presence of an eavesdropper is always detectable, providing a level of trust that classical systems cannot match.

Future-Proof

Because it does not rely on computational hardness, QKD is secure against all future advances in computing, including the development of more powerful quantum computers.
